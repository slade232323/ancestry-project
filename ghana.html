Ghana: The Gold Coast

Introduction

Ghana, known as the Gold Coast during colonial times, is a country rich in history and natural resources. It is famous for its role in the transatlantic slave trade and its vibrant cultural heritage.

Heritage and Culture

Ghana's culture is deeply rooted in its history and traditions. The Ashanti Kingdom is one of the most well-known, with a rich history of gold craftsmanship and elaborate ceremonies. English is the official language, and Christianity is the predominant religion.

Notable Locations

  • Cape Coast Castle: A UNESCO World Heritage site, Cape Coast Castle was a major outpost during the transatlantic slave trade and now serves as a museum.
  • Kakum National Park: Known for its canopy walkway, Kakum National Park is a tropical rainforest home to diverse wildlife and plant species.

Economic and Social Overview

Ghana's economy is driven by gold mining, cocoa production, and oil. The country is one of the world's largest producers of cocoa and has recently seen growth in its oil and gas sectors.
